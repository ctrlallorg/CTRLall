    Pages and Views

    ⭐ Beginner Level

    You may need to adjust how you display your document's page(s) while editing it. This article goes through the basics of zooming and altering page views in Word.

    View tab

    Under the View tab you have several options to zoom and have choose whether to have one or more pages shown on screen.

    Zoom

    The first option to talk about is Zoom. But instead of clicking on the button above within View > Zoom, let's look at the zoom scroller as it's quicker to use; it's found in the bottom right of the screen on the status bar discussed in the Getting started with Word article.

    There are other ways to zoom as well:

    • If you're using a mouse, the fastest way, and the way I'd recommend zooming for ease, is to hold down Ctrl + slide the mouse wheel up to zoom in and Ctrl + slide the mouse wheel down to zoom out. If you don't have a mouse some laptop mousepads offer this function, normally down one side of the mousepad.

    Otherwise there's these keyboard shortcuts you can zoom with:

    • Ctrl and + to zoom in
    • Ctrl and - to zoom out
    • Ctrl + 0 (zero) will reset zoom level to 100%

    Zoom options

    You can find zoom options by clicking on the percentage on the right of the zoom scroller (found bottom right of the screen), or by clicking the Zoom button under View > Zoom shown above.

    There are a range of zoom options letting you choose how to display the page(s). 100% is the standard zoom level for editing. Here you can choose between options to zoom via a percentage or to the page width or to see the whole page. I wouldn't recommend using Many pages unless you're looking for a page that is easily recognisable viewed alongside multiple pages. This option is explored in the intermediate version of this article if you want to understand it better. Text width as well is very zoomed in and not ideal for editing a document at length.

    One Page and Multiple Pages

    Under View > Zoom you can also choose whether to display a single page or multiple pages when you're zoomed out far enough.

    One page view: Use this when you want to focus on one page at a time which you'll typically be doing. This is the default setting.

    Multiple pages view: This lets you see multiple pages side by side when you zoom out far enough. When you zoom into a page it will snap to showing you just that page.

    Layout tab

    Under Layout you can change the size and orientation of a page. You can also alter margins, columns and apply other tools like hyphenation page breaks and section breaks which are a little more advanced.

    View tab

    Under the View tab there are options around zoom and options to choose the number of pages shown on screen.

    Zoom

    Before thinking about using Zoom options under the View tab, first consider these methods for zooming:

    1. The fastest and easiest way to zoom in and out is via Ctrl + sliding mouse wheel up/down. This is what I use and recommend.
    2. Second preference is the zoom slider on the status bar in the far bottom right corner.
    3. Following this there are also some useful shortcuts to be aware of:
      • Ctrl and + to zoom in
      • Ctrl and - to zoom out
      • Ctrl + 0 (zero) will reset zoom level to 100%

      4. Zoom options (via View tab)

      Now if you click on Zoom in the View tab you get a dialog of Zoom options. Most of these are pretty self-explanatory and allow you to be specific if you want to.

      One that needs a little explanation is the Many pages option on the right. Once selected click the monitor icon drop down and you get a small menu to choose a limited number of multiple pages to have displayed. In the example below I've chosen 2 x 4 Pages, so Word will show me 8 pages.

      Note: You can see more pages if you zoom out further. It's debatable how useful this is when you can just hold Ctrl and slide your wheel mouse to the number of pages you want shown.

      Page views

      One page view: One page shown no matter how much you zoom in or out.

      Multiple pages view: At higher zoom level shows multiple pages side by side which can be useful for longer structual editing. If you zoom into a page, the view will snap to a one page view to show more detail. On zooming out again it will show multiple pages.

      Layout tab

      Under Layout you can change the size and orientation of a page. You can also alter margins, columns and other specific layout options like breaks, hyphenation and line numbers.

      Hyphenation

      By default Word disables hyphenation for ease of reading. The Hyphenation menu gives you a few options to choose from. Note: Normally for hypenation to work you'd need to set the text alignment to Justified first.

      It's set to None by default, but if you select Automatic then Word applies hypenation to the whole document, here's an example:

      If you select Manual, Word gives you the option of exactly where to hyphenate a word more specifically:

      Select Hyphenation options and you can determine some greater control

      • Hyphenate words in CAPS (all capitals)
      • Limit consective hyphens to a set number — this prevents too many lines in a row from ending with a hyphenated word. E.g. If you select '2' Word won't allow no more than 2 consecutive lines to end with a hyphen. If a third line would need one, Word adjusts spacing instead.
      • Hyphenation zone: Ignore this one, it's a legacy option from Word 2010 and earlier. This is to do with the amount of white space allowed between the end of the last word and the right margin before a hyphen was inserted. Modern versions of Word manage this automatically.
