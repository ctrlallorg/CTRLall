<h1>Selecting text</h1>

<article class="difficulty-wrapper" role="article" aria-label="Selecting text tutorial with beginner and intermediate versions">
<div id="article-badge" class="article-badge beginner">⭐ Beginner Level</div>

<section class="section-light">
Here's the basics of selecting text in Microsoft Word.
<h2>Select text by click and drag</h2>
Hold down <strong>left click</strong> over a word on your mouse or mousepad and then <strong>drag</strong> the mouse cursor across the word(s) you want to select. Note below how you can drag down/up across rows too:
<img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting text click and drag.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting text by click and drag in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
<h2>Select text by double-clicking</h2>
You can select a whole word by <strong>double clicking</strong> on a word with your left mouse button or mousepad:
<img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting text word double click.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting text word by double clicking in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
To select a whole paragraph you can <strong>triple click</strong> on a word in it:
<img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting text paragraph triple click.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting text paragraph by triple clicking in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
<h2>Selecting punctuation</h2>
When it comes to punctuation marks like commas and full stops/periods, it's easier to select them depending on where they are in a sentence/paragraph. 
<h3>Punctuation in the middle of a sentence</h3>
If the punctuation mark is in the middle of a sentence, <strong>double click</strong> just to the left of the punctuation symbol or on it (Note: when selected it will include the space after it). Be careful positioning the cursor before you click as it can get quite finicky in such a small space; too much to the right and you'll select a word or character that comes after it instead, take a look:
<img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting punctuation mid sentence.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting punctuation in the middle of a sentence in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
<h3>Punctuation at the end of a sentence</h3>
For selecting punctuation at the end of a sentence sometimes it's easier to <strong>click and drag</strong> over it rather than <strong>double click</strong> as you have to be very exact. Some finishing punctuation is coupled together when selected e.g. full stops/periods with quotation marks, take a look:
<img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Select full stop period next to quotation mark.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting a full stop period next to a quotation mark in Word" style="max-width:175px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">

 <section class="section-light">

  <h2>Selecting text in Word</h2>
  <p>You probably already know how to select a word by double clicking on it or dragging over it but here's a few others in case you didn't know them:</p>

  <h3>Triple click to select a paragraph</h3>
  <img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting text paragraph triple click.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting text paragraph by triple clicking in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">

  <h3>Select a row of text via the margin</h3>
  <p>You're less likely to use this but if you need to select a row(s) of text, try clicking the margin space to the left of the row of text and it will select that whole row.</p>
  <p><u>Note</u>: Clicking and dragging in the margin is a slightly faster and easier way to select multiple rows of text than clicking and dragging over that text. Remember you're selecting the whole row though so this won't be what you'd use to select a sentence in the middle of a paragraph.</p> 
  <img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Selecting text margin.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting a row of text by clicking the margin in Word" style="max-width:900px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">

  <h2>Select multiple adjacent text</h2>
  <p>A quick way to select a large amount of adjacent text is to hold down place the insertion point at the start of text, then hold <strong>Shift</strong> and click to the end of what you want to select. Word selects it all in one.</p>
  <img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Select text Shift.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting multiple text with Shift in Word" style="max-width:600px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">


  <h2>Select multiple non-adjacent text</h2>
  <p>This can be quite handy. Hold down <strong>Ctrl</strong> and select multiple content that isn't next to each other. You can use this to cut and paste or copy and paste elsewhere, see <a href="/content-control/copying-content/">copying content</a> and <a href="/content-control/moving-content/">moving content</a> for examples.</p>
  <img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Selecting text/Select text Ctrl.gif' | url }}" alt="Selecting multiple non-adjacent text with Ctrl in Word" style="max-width:600px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
