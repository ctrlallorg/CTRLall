<h1>Pages and Views</h1>

<article class="difficulty-wrapper" role="article" aria-label="Pages and Views tutorial with beginner and intermediate versions">
<div id="article-badge" class="article-badge beginner">⭐ Beginner Level</div>
<!-- Beginner version -->
<div class="difficulty beginner-version"  aria-label="Beginner introduction to Microsoft Word interface and basic page and view options">

  <section class="section-light">
  <p>You may need to adjust how you display your document's page(s) while editing it. This article goes through the basics of zooming and altering page views in Word.</p> 

  <h2>View tab</h2>
  <p>Under the <strong>View</strong> tab you have several options to zoom and have choose whether to have one or more pages shown on screen.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom on Ribbon.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab with zoom options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:250px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h3>Zoom</h3>
  <p>The first option to talk about is <strong>Zoom</strong>. But instead of clicking on the button above within <strong>View > Zoom</strong>, let's look at the zoom scroller as it's quicker to use; it's found in the bottom right of the screen on the <strong>status bar</strong> discussed in the <a href="/word/getting-started-word/">Getting started with Word</a> article.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom slider.png' | url }}"  alt="Zoom slider on statu bar in Word" style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p>There are other ways to zoom as well:</p>
  <ul>
      <li>If you're using a mouse, the fastest way, and <u>the way I'd recommend zooming</u> for ease, is to hold down <strong>Ctrl + slide the mouse wheel up</strong> to zoom in and <strong>Ctrl + slide the mouse wheel down</strong> to zoom out. If you don't have a mouse some laptop mousepads offer this function, normally down one side of the mousepad.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Otherwise there's these keyboard shortcuts you can zoom with:</p>
  <ul>
      <li><strong>Ctrl and +</strong> to zoom in <img class="thumbnail left" src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl++.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl and + shortcut: Zoom in"  style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
      <li><strong>Ctrl and -</strong> to zoom out<img class="thumbnail left"  src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+-.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl and - shortcut: Zoom out"  style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
      <li><strong>Ctrl + 0 (zero)</strong> will reset zoom level to 100%<img class="thumbnail left"  src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+0.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl + 0 shortcut: reset zoom level"  style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
  </ul>


  <h3>Zoom options</h3>
  <p>You can find zoom options by clicking on the <strong>percentage</strong> on the right of the zoom scroller (found bottom right of the screen), or by clicking the <strong>Zoom</strong> button under <strong>View > Zoom</strong> shown above.</p>
  <img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom options via slider.png' | url }}"  alt="Zoom options via zoom scroller in Word" style="max-width:225px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">
  <img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom on Ribbon Zoom option.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab Zoom options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:250px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle">



  <p>There are a range of zoom options letting you choose how to display the page(s). 100% is the standard zoom level for editing. Here you can choose between options to zoom via a <strong>percentage</strong> or to the <strong>page width</strong> or to see the <strong>whole page</strong>. I wouldn't recommend using <strong>Many pages</strong> unless you're looking for a page that is easily recognisable viewed alongside multiple pages. This option is explored in the <a href="?difficulty=intermediate">intermediate version</a> of this article if you want to understand it better. <strong>Text width</strong> as well is very zoomed in and not ideal for editing a document at length.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom.png' | url }}"  alt="Zoom tool options in Word" style="max-width:300px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h3>One Page and Multiple Pages</h3>  
  <p>Under <strong>View > Zoom</strong> you can also choose whether to display a single page or multiple pages when you're zoomed out far enough.</p>
  <p><strong>One page view</strong>: Use this when you want to focus on one page at a time which you'll typically be doing. This is the default setting.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/One Page.png' | url }}"  alt="One page view in Word" style="max-width:500px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p><strong>Multiple pages view</strong>: This lets you see multiple pages side by side when you zoom out far enough. When you zoom into a page it will snap to showing you just that page.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Multiple Pages.png' | url }}"  alt="Mulitple pages view in Word" style="max-width:500px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h2>Layout tab</h2>
  <p>Under <strong>Layout</strong> you can change the <strong>size</strong> and <strong>orientation</strong> of a page. You can also alter <strong>margins</strong>, <strong>columns</strong> and apply other tools like <strong>hyphenation</strong> page breaks and section breaks which are a little more advanced.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Layout Page Setup.png' | url }}"  alt="Layout tab options on ribbon in Word" style="max-width:350px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p><strong>Size</strong> and <strong>Orientation</strong> are the most common tools you 'll use here:</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Orientation and Size.png' | url }}"  alt="Page Orientation and Size options in Word" style="max-width:450px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <section class="section-light">
  This article explains the basics of altering pages and views in Microsoft Word.

  <h2>View tab</h2>
  <p>Under the View tab there are options around zoom and options to choose the number of pages shown on screen.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom on Ribbon.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab with zoom options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:275px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  
  <h3>Zoom</h3>
  <p>Before thinking about using <strong>Zoom</strong> options under the <strong>View</strong> tab, first consider these methods for zooming:</p>
  <ol>
  <li>The fastest and easiest way to zoom in and out is via <strong>Ctrl + sliding mouse wheel up/down</strong>. This is what I use and recommend.</li>
  <li>Second preference is the zoom slider on the status bar in the far bottom right corner.
    <img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom slider.png' | url }}"  alt="Zoom slider on statu bar in Word" style="max-width:250px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
  <li>Following this there are also some useful shortcuts to be aware of:
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Ctrl and +</strong> to zoom in <img class="thumbnail left" src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl++.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl and + shortcut: Zoom in" style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
    <li><strong>Ctrl and -</strong> to zoom out<img class="thumbnail left"  src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+-.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl and - shortcut: Zoom out" style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
    <li><strong>Ctrl + 0 (zero)</strong> will reset zoom level to 100%<img class="thumbnail left"  src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+0.png' | url }}" alt="Ctrl + 0 shortcut: reset zoom level"  style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></li>
  </ul>
  </li>

  <h3>Zoom options (via View tab)</h3>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom on Ribbon Zoom option.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab Zoom options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:275px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>Now if you click on <strong>Zoom</strong> in the <strong>View</strong> tab you get a dialog of <strong>Zoom options</strong>. Most of these are pretty self-explanatory and allow you to be specific if you want to.</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab with zoom options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:350px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>One that needs a little explanation is the <strong>Many pages</strong> option on the right. Once selected click the monitor icon drop down and you get a small menu to choose a limited number of multiple pages to have displayed. In the example below I've chosen <strong>2 x 4 Pages</strong>, so Word will show me 8 pages.</p>
  <p><u>Note</u>: You can see more pages if you zoom out further. It's debatable how useful this is when you can just hold <strong>Ctrl</strong> and slide your wheel mouse to the number of pages you want shown.</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom many pages.png' | url }}"  alt="Zoom options many pages option in Word" style="max-width:400px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Many pages 2 x 4.gif' | url }}"  alt="Many pages option demonstration in Word" style="max-width:1000px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  
  <h3>Page views</h3>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Zoom on Ribbon Pages options.png' | url }}"  alt="View tab Pages options on Ribbon in Word" style="max-width:275px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p><strong>One page</strong> view: One page shown no matter how much you zoom in or out.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/One Page.png' | url }}"  alt="One page view in Word" style="max-width:500px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p><strong>Multiple pages</strong> view: At higher zoom level shows multiple pages side by side which can be useful for longer structual editing. If you zoom into a page, the view will snap to a one page view to show more detail. On zooming out again it will show multiple pages.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Multiple Pages.png' | url }}"  alt="Mulitple pages view in Word" style="max-width:500px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h2>Layout tab</h2>
  <p>Under <strong>Layout</strong> you can change the <strong>size</strong> and <strong>orientation</strong> of a page. You can also alter <strong>margins</strong>, <strong>columns</strong> and other specific layout options like <strong>breaks</strong>, <strong>hyphenation</strong> and <strong>line numbers</strong>.</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Layout Page Setup.png' | url }}"  alt="Layout tab options on ribbon in Word" style="max-width:400px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p><strong>Size</strong> and <strong>Orientation</strong> are the most common tools used here:</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Orientation and Size.png' | url }}"  alt="Page Orientation and Size options in Word" style="max-width:500px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>Regarding size you can even customise the page size with <strong>More paper sizes</strong>. This will bring up the <strong>Page setup</strong> dialog which is explored in this article: <a href="/word/page-setup-options/">Page setup options</a>.</p>

  <h3>Breaks</h3>
  <p><span class="glossary-hover" data-term="page-break">Page breaks</span> and <span class="glossary-hover" data-term="section-break">section breaks</span> are explored in these articles: <ul>
    <li><a href="/word/page-breaks/">Page breaks</a></li>
    <li><a href="/word/section-breaks/">Section breaks</a></li>
  </ul>
  
  <h3>Line numbers</h3>
  <p>Line numbers are explored in this article: <a href="">Line numbering</a>.</p>

  <h3>Hyphenation</h3>
  <p>By default Word disables hyphenation for ease of reading. The Hyphenation menu gives you a few options to choose from. <u>Note</u>: Normally for hypenation to work you'd need to set the text alignment to <strong>Justified</strong> first.</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail left border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Hyphenation menu.png' | url }}"  alt="Hyphenation menu in Word" style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>It's set to <strong>None</strong> by default, but if you select <strong>Automatic</strong> then Word applies hypenation to the whole document, here's an example:</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Hyphenation example.png' | url }}"  alt="Page Orientation and Size options in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>If you select <strong>Manual</strong>, Word gives you the option of exactly where to hyphenate a word more specifically:</p>

  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Hyphenation manual.png' | url }}"  alt="Page Orientation and Size options in Word" style="max-width:650px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>Select <strong>Hyphenation options</strong> and you can determine some greater control</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border left" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Pages and Views/Hyphenation options.png' | url }}"  alt="Page Orientation and Size options in Word" style="max-width:275px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <ul>
    <li><u>Hyphenate words in CAPS</u> (all capitals)</li>
    <li><u>Limit consective hyphens to</u> a set number — this prevents too many lines in a row from ending with a hyphenated word. E.g. If you select '2' Word won't allow no more than 2 consecutive lines to end with a hyphen. If a third line would need one, Word adjusts spacing instead.</li>
    <li><u>Hyphenation zone</u>: Ignore this one, it's a legacy option from Word 2010 and earlier. This is to do with the amount of white space allowed between the end of the last word and the right margin before a hyphen was inserted. Modern versions of Word manage this automatically.</li>
  </ul>
