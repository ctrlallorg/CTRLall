<h1>Page breaks</h1>

<section class="section-light">
<h2>What are page breaks?</h2>
<p>Page breaks are where you draw an invisible line on the page and shift all content after it over to the next page. You may have seen a document where there's a lot of white space on a page going to the bottom, in most cases it's likely a page break has been added.</p>
<p>If you're just looking to get rid of one in a document you have, go here: <a href="#remove-page-break-beg">How to remove page breaks</a>.</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Page break 2 pages annotated.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break over two pages" style="max-width:600px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
<h4>Why would you want to add a page break?</h4>
<p>Adding in page breaks let you decide if the content after it should move down one page. You might want to do this for layout reasons, such as starting the next chapter on a new page.</p>

<h2 id="insert-page-break-beg">How to insert a page break</h2>
<p>The easiest way to insert a page break is to press <strong>Ctrl + Enter</strong> on your keyboard.</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail left" src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+Enter.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break over two pages" style="max-width:200px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
<p>Once pressed, all content after the page break shifts to the next page.</p>


<h2 id="remove-page-break-beg">How to remove a page break</h2>

<p>Page breaks are invisible. To see them you'll first need to select <strong>Home > Paragraph > Show/Hide ¶</strong>. This turns on formatting marks that are usually hidden.</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Show Hide non-printing formatting marks.png' | url }}"  alt="Show Hide non-printing formatting marks on ribbon in Word" style="max-width:250px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
<p>A page break will show up looking like this:</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Page break 2 pages.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break highlighted across two pages" style="max-width:800px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
<p>To delete, simply drag over page break to select it then press <strong>Backspace</strong> or <strong>Delete</strong>.</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Deleting page break.png' | url }}"  alt="Selecting a page break in Word" style="max-width:450px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>
<p>Once the page break is deleted, content from the next page will shift back like this:</p>
<p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Deleting page break.gif' | url }}"  alt="Demonstration deleting page break in Word" style="max-width:800px; width:100%; height:auto; vertical-align:middle"></p>

<div class="difficulty intermediate-version" aria-label="Intermediate article exploring page breaks" style="display:none;">

  <section class="section-light">
  <p>Page breaks are useful for organising the layout and flow of your document. They are a formatting marker where all content after them shifts onto the following page, ‘breaking’ from content onto the next page.</p>
  <p>Word has automatic page breaks at the bottom of each page for when your content exceeds the bottom margin; at that point content is pushed to the next page. Page breaks become useful when manually added; you can add them on any line in a Word document.</p> 
  <p>Unwanted page breaks however can be annoying if you're unfamiliar with them or you have lots of them in a document. If you need to get rid of page breaks see this section: <a href="#remove-page-break">How do I remove a page break?</a></p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Page break 2 pages annotated.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break over two pages" style="width:90%; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h4>So why would you want to have a page break? Or why does the document you’re working with have them?</h4>
  <p>It could one of a few reasons: a way of structuring content in terms of topics or sections/units/chapters; to avoiding awkward <span class="glossary-hover" data-term="orphan">orphan</span>/<span class="glossary-hover" data-term="widow">widow</span> splits or headings separated from content that follows after; as a means to keep certain content on one page like an image or table; for printing purposes; or maybe just sheer aesthetic preference.</p>
  <p>However if a document is edited they can become an unwanted problem that needs removing. If you need to remove them see: <a href="#remove-page-break">How do you remove a page break?</a></p>

  <h4>I can’t find the page break</h4>
  <p>That’s because they’re invisible by default. In order to see them you need to turn on formatting marks (also known as non-printing characters) which you can do via <strong>Home > Show/Hide ¶</strong>:</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Show Hide non-printing formatting marks.png' | url }}"  alt="Show Hide non-printing formatting marks on ribbon in Word" style="width:33%; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Page break 2 pages.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break highlighted across two pages" style="width:90%; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <h2 id="insert-page-break">How do you add a page break?</h2>
  <p>On the ribbon: <strong>Insert > Page Break</strong></p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Insert page break.png' | url }}"  alt="Insert Page break on ribbon in Word" style="width:25%; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p>or using keyboard shortcut: <strong>Ctrl + Enter</strong>.</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail" src="{{ '/assets/images/keyboard shortcuts/Ctrl+Enter.png' | url }}"  alt="Ctrl+Enter shortcut: insert page break" style="width:30%; vertical-align:middle"></p>
  <p></p>

  <h2 id="remove-page-break">How do you remove a page break?</h2>
  <p>If you know where the page break is you can just simply select a line roughly after it and press Backspace until you delete it, but since it's invisible by default you will be doing this blind until you see the content jump up.</p>
  <p>It’s much easier to turn on formatting marks (<strong>Home > Show/Hide ¶</strong>) and then you’ll see page break appear like this:</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Page break.png' | url }}"  alt="Page break on page in Word" style="width:90%; vertical-align:middle"></p>

  <p>Now you can specifically select the page break and delete it like regular text. Once deleted content will jump up to that page like this:</p>
  <p><img class="thumbnail border" src="{{ '/assets/images/word/Page breaks/Deleting page break.gif' | url }}"  alt="Demonstration deleting page break in Word" style="width:90%; vertical-align:middle"></p>


  <h2>Other kinds of breaks in Word</h2>
  <ul>
  <li><strong>Section breaks</strong> – Break a document up into sections in which you can apply different formatting, styles, headers and footers, page size and orientation, without affecting the rest of the document. For more see <a href="/word/section-breaks/">Section breaks</a>.</li>
  <li><strong>Line breaks (<span class="glossary-hover" data-term="soft-return">soft return</span>)</strong> – Moves text to the next line without start a new paragraph.</li>
  <li><strong>Column breaks</strong> – These are used in multi-column layouts to shift content to the next column.</li>
  </ul>
